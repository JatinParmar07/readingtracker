¶ 01
The human eye makes rapid, jerky movements called saccades as it scans a line of text. Between each saccade, the eye pauses briefly in a fixation, during which visual information is extracted.
¶ 02
Eye tracking technology reveals how readers allocate cognitive resources across a passage. Longer fixations on a word suggest greater processing difficulty, while regressions indicate the reader has returned to reprocess earlier content.
¶ 03
Skilled readers produce fewer, shorter fixations and fewer regressions compared to novice readers. This pattern reflects the automaticity of word recognition developed through extensive reading practice.
